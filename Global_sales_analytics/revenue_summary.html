Revenue Dashboard Overview

Dashboard Objective

This dashboard analyzes revenue performance between 2014 and 2021 across regions, product categories, sales channels, and time periods. The objective is to identify revenue drivers, growth opportunities, and business segments contributing most to overall sales performance.

Quarterly Revenue Performance

Quarterly analysis reveals clear differences in revenue contribution throughout the year:

  1. Q2 (Apr-Jun) - 26.94%
    The strongest performing quarter, driven by exceptional performance in May (9.51%) and June (9.03%). This period represents the peak revenue season and should be a key focus for marketing and inventory planning.
  2. Q3 (Jul-Sep) - 26.54%
    The second-highest contributing quarter, supported by strong sales in July (9.42%) and August (9.30%). Revenue remained stable despite a slowdown in September.
  3. Q1 (Jan-Mar) - 25.69%
    A solid start to the year with balanced performance across all three months. March delivered the strongest contribution within the quarter at 8.93%.
  4. Q4 (Oct-Dec) - 22.78%
    The lowest-performing quarter, with all three months contributing below 8%. This suggests weaker demand during the final quarter and highlights an opportunity for targeted sales initiatives and promotional campaigns.

Key Business Insights

    • Europe generated the highest revenue, contributing approximately 26% of total sales and establishing itself as the company's largest market.
    • Household and Office Supplies were the strongest revenue-generating categories, each exceeding $2 billion in sales.
    • Online and Offline channels contributed nearly equally, reducing dependence on a single sales channel.
    • Q2 and Q3 generated more than 53% of annual revenue, highlighting the middle of the year as the strongest sales period.
    • May was the highest-performing month, contributing 9.51% of total revenue.
